    Research Experience

    Graduate Research Assistant at ASU-Mayo Center for Innovative Imaging

  • Implemented machine learning tools (random forest and SVM) on real and simulated building data to explore the degree of similarity between real and simulated building, and applied statistical inferences (KS test) on both data for feature selection.
  • Proposed a novel metric termed Eigen-Entropy (EE), based on information entropy for high-dimensional data, for decision making to measure data homogeneity/heterogeneity.
  • Developed an oversampling algorithm based on Eigen-Entropy (EE-SMOTE) for multiclass imbalanced learning, which significantly improved classification accuracy by nearly 50% compared to traditional approaches.
  • Developed an EE-based heterogeneous sampling algorithm to construct baselines (ground truths) for building fault detection, which was able to reach 100% fault detection rate by capturing heterogeneous samples. This research paper was selected one of four finalists in Best Paper Competition at 2021 IISE Annual Conference & Expo (DAIS Track).
  • Collaborated in a github package development for commercialized building fault detection and diagnosis.
    • 2018 - Present

    Research Assistant at The Cardiovascular Institute of New Jersey

  • Performed statistical analysis using R on Personalized Disease Networks (PDN) for understanding and predicting cardiovascular diseases, which included generation on network matrix for dataset of comorbidity in New Jersey from 2011 to 2014, determination on the number of clusters of datasets by 3 different methods (Hierarchical clustering, Silhoutte, Gap statistics), which provided a guideline on dimension reduction on PDN for further data analysis.
